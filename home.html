The Spring Bud Project was launched in 1989; since then, it has undergone significant development and, more importantly, it has had immeasurable effects on the lives of many girls across China. How? With donations from people from all segments of society, the project has helped the girls —— who had to drop out due to lack of money —— return to school. They have felt the great love of and care shown by everyone who has donated their time and/or money to the project. The benefactors' love must never been forgotten. In 1989, the board of the China Children and Tenagers' Fund (CCTF) agreed to establish a fund with the specific purpose of paying the tuition so impoverished girls could return to school. It was the first fund of its kind in China. In 1992, the fund, commonly referred to as the "fund for girls' tuition," was officially named the Spring Bud Project.

We are finding some volunteers who are passionate about children’s education. Volunteers will go to Guangxi and stay with under-privileged children for one weeks. During the one week, volunteers will be their teacher to have classes. We hope that our volunteers can bring a bigger world to the children, broaden their minds and enrich their knowledge.
